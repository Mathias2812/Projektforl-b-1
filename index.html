Why is plastic waste such a struggle in these countries?

The bar chart reveals that both the Solomon Islands and Suriname mismanage 100% of their plastic waste, with Mozambique, Madagascar, and Uganda following closely behind. This indicates that all plastic waste produced in these countries ends up as litter, in landfills, or in the ocean.


Additionally, the world map highlights that African and other developing nations generally have high MWI scores, significantly contributing to the global plastic problem. However, the rest of the world generally scores low, hinting that they might have better resources to manage their plastic waste. Consequently, it appears there is still some way to go to achieve the UN's 2030 goal of reducing environmental impact by focusing on improved waste management.
